About Me

Hi, I’m Aninda Pal, a PhD researcher at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur working under Professor Ritwik Ghoshal. When I’m not doing research, I love reading books, playing table tennis, and jogging—they keep me curious, active, and balanced.

My research explores how structures interact with fluids, especially in challenging conditions like underwater environments or high-intensity loading. I work on developing semi-analytical and computational models to understand vibrations, acoustics, and impact response of advanced composite and metastructured plates. Ultimately, I’m interested in creating ideas that can help design better systems for underwater vehicles, robotics, defense, and marine engineering.

Research Interests

  • Fluid–structure interaction under shock, blast, and transient loading
  • Vibroacoustics of plates, shells, and metastructured composites
  • Noise and vibration control in marine and underwater systems
  • Nonlinear structural dynamics and wave propagation
  • Bioinspired and metamaterial-based design for vibration and acoustic performance

Beyond Research

I enjoy exploring the connections between physics and engineering through computational modeling and analytical frameworks, while also being motivated by practical applications in underwater vehicles, robotics, defense, and marine infrastructure. I am keen on multidisciplinary collaboration and on developing models that bridge fundamental mechanics with real-world engineering needs.
